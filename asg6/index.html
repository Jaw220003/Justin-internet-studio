Green Hat

Thoughts

Creative energy flowed during the Green Hat phase, where participants brainstormed alternatives, new strategies, and unconventional approaches. They looked beyond the original idea, considering how it could be improved or approached from different angles. No suggestion was too wild or impractical during this stage. This led to some breakthrough thinking, including a hybrid solution that combined elements of two competing ideas. The group felt energized by the possibility of innovation and progress.

Blue Hat

Thoughts

Under the Blue Hat, the group took a step back to review the entire discussion. They assessed how each hat had contributed to their understanding and mapped out a clear process moving forward. The facilitator summarized key insights from each perspective, acknowledged unresolved issues, and assigned action items. There was also a discussion about how to revisit the topic once new data became available. The Blue Hat provided structure, closure, and a plan for next steps, ensuring the conversation remained focused and productive.
