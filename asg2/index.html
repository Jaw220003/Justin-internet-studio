What is the difference between structured content and semantic HTML

  • Structred content resulted in ambiguous or poorly organized code. Semantic HTML5 Elements, on the other hand,
    introduces a range of specific elements designed to express the semantic meaning of different parts of a webpage

How do they work together?

  • Structured content and semantic HTML work together by providing both a meaningful organization of information
    and the technical markup to convey that meaning to browsers, assistive technologies, and search engines.

Why is it important to have structured content and semantic HTML in your web pages?

  • Enhance accessibility for users with disabilities and assistive technologies, improve SEO for search engine understanding
    and indexing, provide better user experience by clearly defining content hierarchy, and make the code itself more readable
    and maintainable for developers.

Reading Response
Banishing Your Inner Critic – Denise Jacobs

Denise Jacobs compares our self-doubt to a troll-like “inner critic” that hinders creativity and productivity. Of the techniques she offers to silence this critic, the most successful one for me would be “naming and externalizing the inner critic.” By giving it a separate identity, I can detach those negative thoughts from myself and view them more objectively. This would help me stop internalizing self-doubt and allow me to move forward with confidence in my creative work.

Procrastination – David McRaney

In the YouTube video, David McRaney explains the concept of “hyperbolic discounting” — the tendency to choose smaller, immediate rewards over larger, delayed rewards. This explains why we often procrastinate, favoring short-term comfort over long-term success. One method McRaney identifies to counteract this is “precommitment”, where you make a decision in advance that locks in your future behavior (like using a website blocker or setting a deadline with consequences). This would work best for me because creating structure and accountability ahead of time helps me avoid temptation and stay focused.

Critical Web Design – Chapter 1: Networks

The chapter outlines four types of power in networks: power over, power to, power with, and power within. As a beginner in web publishing, I have some “power to” — the ability to create and publish my own content online. This gives me agency in shaping how my voice is represented on the web. However, my influence (or “power over”) a broader audience or system is currently limited. As I learn more and connect with others, I hope to gain more “power with” and “power within” through collaboration and confidence in my own digital identity.
